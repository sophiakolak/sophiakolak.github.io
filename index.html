Research

I am interested in a wide variety of computer science topics, from robotics, machine learning, and artificial intelligence to software engineering and automatic program repair. I am also broadly interested in neuroscience and philosophy of science.

Previously, I worked in the Axel Lab Axel Lab under PI and Nobel Laureate Richard Axel. The lab's goal is to understand how the brain is able to construct an internal representation of reality. My project was to investigate how the olfactory circuit in particular impacts our mental represntation of distance, time, and space. Most of my work involved using recurrent neural networks and deep learning algorithms to find trends in high dimensionality neuron spiking data.

I also participated in the Software Engineering REU program at Carnegie Mellon University this summer. I was advised by Claire Le Goues and Michael Hilton, and worked alongisde Chris Timperley and Afsoon Afzal, studying the Robot Operating System (ROS). Our original idea was to understand the state of testing in robotics code, since very little research had been done on the subject. As I collected more an more data on ROS, however, it became clear that just the software ecosystem alone was incredibly interesting and deserved its own research paper. We are currently still working on a paper that details all of our findings, and I gave a talk on this research at ROScon in October. Video and slides can be found here!
